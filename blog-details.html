The 5 Things You Must Pack Away Before Taking Listing Photos

If potential buyers are swiping right past your home on real estate apps, the issue might be what's in the background, not the house itself.

"You never get a second chance to make a first impression, and in modern real estate, that first impression happens on a 6-inch screen."

It's a familiar story: a homeowner decides to sell, hires an agent, and schedules photography. On photo day, the house is clean—vaccuumed, floors mopped, beds made. But when the listing goes live, the photos feel cluttered, small, and uninspired.

The difference between a clean house and a staged house is monumental. Cleaning removes dirt; staging removes distractions. Before the photographer arrives, ensure these five categories of items are completely packed away into labeled moving boxes.

1. Personal Photographs

We love seeing photos of families celebrating trips, graduations, and holidays. But buyers don't want to see your family; they want to visualize their own. Leaving personal photos up actively reminds buyers that this is someone else's territory.

2. Small Kitchen Appliances

Toasters, blenders, coffee makers, block knives, and air fryers must leave the countertops. Yes, even if you use them every day. Clearing the counters creates an illusion of expansive prep space. If buyers see counters cluttered with appliances, their immediate psychological takeaway is "this kitchen lacks storage."

Pro Tip: Kitchen Sinks

Remove dishrags, sponges, dish soap bottles, and drain stoppers. The sink should look like a pristine showroom display.

3. The Refrigerator Gallery

Magnets, report cards, takeaway menus, and wedding invitations clustered on the side of a refrigerator immediately pull focus in wide-angle photos. A clear surface instantly upgrades the perception of the appliance and the kitchen as a whole.

4. Daily Toiletries

Bathrooms should evoke a high-end spa, not a busy morning routine. Remove toothbrushes, toothpaste, razors, half-empty shampoo bottles, loofahs, and trash cans. Replace them with rolled white towels, an orchid, or high-end bottled hand soap.

5. Excess "Floor Decor"

Magazine racks, excessive floor plants, ottomans that have no matching chair, decorative baskets, and pet beds eat up floor space. When taking photos, the more continuous flooring visible, the larger a room feels.
